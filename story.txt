**Chapter One: Wings of Dust**

The first thing I ever piloted was a drone cobbled together from scrap—half its frame scavenged from the ruins of old cargo haulers, the other half from some pre-war drone no one had ever bothered to identify. I named it *Dustwing*. Fitting, given it barely held together.

The second thing I piloted was a starfighter.

Well, "piloted" might be generous. I crashed it straight into the jagged remains of the orbital shield. Turns out, commandeering a warship in the middle of a planetary invasion isn't the ideal way to learn flight mechanics.

But then, nothing about my life had been ideal.

"Amara, they're breaking through the outer colonies." Commander Briel's voice crackled over the ship's intercom as I sat frozen in the cockpit, my hands trembling over the controls. "The Legion doesn't stop. They don't negotiate. They're coming for us, and you're wasting time. Can you fly or not?"

I glanced at the viewscreen, where a hulking alien destroyer loomed like a shadow of death, blotting out the stars. It was surrounded by a swarm of enemy fighters—sleek and agile, their crimson engines pulsing like the heartbeat of a predator.

The answer was obvious. I couldn't fly. I hadn't been through training like the others in the fleet. I wasn't born into the ranks of the Dominion's elite pilots. I'd grown up in the slums of Outpost Nine, stealing food scraps and dodging enforcers who thought orphans like me were better off scrubbing air ducts than breathing free air.

But here I was, strapped into the cockpit of a prototype fighter, because there were no more trained pilots left.

Because we were losing.

"Amara!" Briel's voice barked in my ear again.

"Yeah, yeah, I can fly," I lied. My hands clenched the controls as adrenaline surged through my veins. "But don't expect me to bring it back in one piece."

The engines roared to life, and the ship shuddered as I punched the throttle. The hangar bay doors opened, revealing the chaos outside—flashes of plasma fire, the brilliant arcs of destruction lancing across the void. For a moment, I hesitated, staring at the battlefield before me.

Then I gritted my teeth and pushed forward. The fighter shot out of the hangar, and I was thrust into the maelstrom.

And for the first time in my life, I felt truly alive.
